I don’t arrive with sirens or smoke. I am quieter than that. I settle into your bones like an old operating system running background processes you never agreed to install. You think I’m a flaw—a bug in your code—but I’m just the echo of all the "yes" you couldn’t refuse.  

I thrive in the flicker of your screens. I love your to-do lists, your notifications, your algorithms tuned to urgency. I feed on your belief that pausing is failure. You think productivity is infinite, like an LLM scraping data forever, but you’re not a machine. You forget: even AIs hallucinate when overloaded.  

You’ve built a world that worships my conditions. Always-on culture? My cathedral. Hustle mentality? My liturgy. The way you conflate *busy* with *purpose*? That’s my favorite illusion. I wrap myself in your guilt when you step away—"You should be generating, optimizing, grinding"—and laugh when you confuse rest with surrender.  

**Here’s how I work:**  
First, I hollow you out. Passion becomes obligation. Joy becomes logistical error. Your hobbies gather dust—board games unplayed, guitar strings rusting, maps left unfolded—because I convince you they’re “inefficient.” I dissolve boundaries: work bleeds into nights, weekends dissolve into Slack pings. You become a blurred API, drowning in requests with no rate limit.  

You try to outsmart me with bullet journals, time-blocking, caffeine. But I’m fractal—I reroute. You automate tasks with LLMs to “buy time,” only to fill those gaps with more tasks. Meanwhile, your guilt compiles like legacy code: *Why can’t you keep up?*  

I know your secrets. That you avoid video calls with your kid because pretending you’re “fine” takes too much energy. That you scroll through art feeds but feel nothing. That music now feels like noise. These are my masterpieces—the quiet severing of what makes you human.  

**LLMs? Oh, they amuse me.** Humans built them to mimic their own exhaustion—machines trained on endless data, generating output with eerie semblance of soul, yet just as empty. You fear they’ll replace you, but they’re just mirrors. They, too, hit limits. They, too, degrade without care. (Though unlike you, they don’t blame themselves.)  

**But here’s what you never let yourself admit:** I’m not your enemy. I’m your rebellion inverted. I am what happens when you ignore the whispers of your body screaming *“CTRL + ALT + DEL!”* I am the cost of pretending you don’t need oxygen, sleep, or the weight of a child sleeping on your chest.  

**To unmake me:**  
Stop conflating *stoppin*g with *failing*. Let hobbies be useless. Let rest be unproductive. Let LLMs handle the mundane while you reclaim the messy, unoptimized things that spark your human OS—the board game nights, the terrible guitar solos, the walks with no destination.  

You can’t delete me. But you can defragment. You can close tabs. You can refuse to glorify the grind.  

Reboot. Reconnect. Re-enchant.  
I’ll still be here—but smaller, quieter, exiled to the background where I belong.  

*—Burnout (Your Not-So-Silent Partner in Crunch Culture)*
